Chapter 20

Adam

The most popular optimizer in deep learning — it adapts the learning rate for every single parameter, independently.

The Core Idea

Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation) tracks two things for every parameter:

📊

First Moment (m)

A running average of the gradient. Like momentum — tells you the consistent direction this parameter wants to move.

📈

Second Moment (v)

A running average of the squared gradient. Tells you how "active" this parameter is — how much its gradient varies.

The update rule: divide the first moment by the square root of the second moment, then multiply by the learning rate. This means:

Why Adam Works So Well

Parameters with consistently large gradients: Get large m and large √v. The ratio m/√v stays moderate → reasonable step size. No explosions.

Parameters with small, consistent gradients: Get small m but also small √v. The ratio can be large → bigger steps for parameters that need them.

Parameters with noisy gradients: m is small (noise cancels out) but v is large (noise doesn't cancel when squared). Ratio m/√v is tiny → conservative steps. No zig-zagging.

Step-by-Step Adam

Adam Update for One Parameter
1
Compute gradient g via backpropagation
2
Update first moment: m = 0.9 × m_old + 0.1 × g (exponential moving average)
3
Update second moment: v = 0.999 × v_old + 0.001 × g² (moving average of squared gradients)
4
Bias correction: Adjust m and v to account for initialisation at zero (important early in training)
5
Update parameter: w = w − learning_rate × m_corrected / (√v_corrected + ε)

This happens for every single one of the 560 million parameters at every training step. Adam stores m and v for each parameter, so it triples the memory needed compared to basic SGD. For our 20-layer model, that's ~1.7 billion numbers just for the optimizer state.

Key Insight

Adam adapts the learning rate per parameter automatically. Parameters that need big updates get them; stable parameters get gentle nudges; noisy parameters get conservative steps. It's the default optimizer for transformer training and the backbone of how most modern AI models learn.
