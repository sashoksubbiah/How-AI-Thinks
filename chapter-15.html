Chapter 15

Rotary Embeddings

How the model knows that "France" in position 4 is different from "France" in position 98 — by rotating vectors based on their position.

Why Position Matters

Word order is critical to meaning. Consider these Tamil sentences with the same words but different meanings:

Tamil Word Order Example

நான் உன்னை நேசிக்கிறேன் = "I love you" (Subject-Object-Verb)

நீ என்னை நேசிக்கிறாய் = "You love me" (completely different meaning!)

The words change form based on who is subject vs object, but position also matters. In English it's even more stark: "Dog bites man" ≠ "Man bites dog" — same words, different order, completely different meaning.

But in the attention mechanism from Chapter 14, a "France" token in position 4 generates the exact same Key and Query as a "France" in position 98 — they go through the same linear transform with the same weights. The model has no way to know where in the sequence a token sits.

The Solution: Rotate the Vectors

Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE), invented by researchers in Shenzhen, China in 2023, solve this elegantly. After computing the Q and K vectors, but before comparing them, we rotate each vector by an angle that depends on its position.

🕐

Clock analogy: Imagine each Q and K vector as a clock hand. Position 1 rotates it a tiny bit. Position 2 rotates it twice as much. Position 100 rotates it 100 times as much. Now even if two tokens have identical embeddings (like "buffalo buffalo buffalo..."), their rotated Q and K vectors will be different, and the attention mechanism can learn position-dependent patterns.

Key Properties of RoPE

🔄

Relative Position

The dot product between two rotated vectors depends on the distance between their positions, not their absolute positions. Token 5 attending to token 3 works the same way as token 105 attending to token 103.

📏

Nearby = Similar

Tokens close together have similar rotations, so their Q-K dot products are naturally higher. Distant tokens have very different rotations. The model can learn to exploit this.

0️⃣

Zero Parameters

RoPE involves no learned parameters! The rotation angles are computed from a fixed formula based on position and dimension. Sophisticated yet free.

♾️

Any Length

Works for sequences of any length — no need to pre-define a maximum. The rotation formula extends naturally to any position number.

Where RoPE Fits in the Architecture

RoPE is applied after the Q and K linear transforms but before the dot-product attention score computation. It does not touch the Values — position information only affects which positions attend to each other, not the information that flows between them.

Key Insight

Rotary embeddings inject position information into the attention mechanism by rotating Query and Key vectors based on their position in the sequence. This lets the model learn that nearby words are more likely to be relevant, that word order matters, and that the same word in different positions can have different roles. All with zero learned parameters.
