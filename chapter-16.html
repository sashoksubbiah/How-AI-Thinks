Chapter 16

The Whole Model

Putting it all together — and counting every single one of the 560,988,160 parameters.

We've now seen every piece. Let's put the complete architecture in one place and do the most satisfying exercise in this guide: count every parameter.

The Full Architecture

Input: B × T token IDs (e.g., 32 sequences × 2048 tokens)
↓ Lookup table
Embedding: B × T × D (32 × 2048 × 1280)
↓ × 20 blocks
Transformer Block:
Norm → Attention(Q,K,V linear + RoPE + mask + multi-head) → +skip → Norm → MLP(Linear→ReLU²→Linear) → +skip
↓ Final norm
Final Linear: B × T × V (32 × 2048 × 65,536)
↓ Softmax → probabilities
Output: next-token probability distributions

Key Insight

You now understand every single component of a GPT model: token embedding, 20 transformer blocks (each with attention + MLP + skip connections + normalization), and a final linear projection. The 20-layer model has exactly 560,988,160 parameters. Most are in the MLP layers (47%) and attention layers (23%), with embeddings and the final linear layer taking up the rest. Norm, ReLU, masking, and RoPE all have zero learned parameters — the magic is in the linear transformations and the clever architecture that connects them.
